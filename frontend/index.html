How to Download YouTube Videos

Three simple steps — no install, no extension, no signup needed.

1
Copy Video URL
Copy the YouTube video link from your browser address bar or use the Share → Copy Link button.
2
Paste & Fetch
Paste the URL into Lumio and click Fetch Info. We'll pull the video title, thumbnail, and available formats.
3
Pick & Save
Choose MP3 or MP4 with your preferred quality, then click Download. Your file saves directly to your device.

Common Issues & Solutions

Ran into a problem? Here's how to fix the most common ones.

Try these solutions:

Verify the YouTube URL is public and valid.
Try the full watch URL instead of a shortened youtu.be redirect.
Retry after a brief wait if source throttling occurs.
Age-restricted, private, or member-only videos may fail.
Refresh and retry in a clean browser session.

Not all YouTube videos are available in every resolution. The original uploader determines maximum quality.

Select the next lower quality that is available.
Older videos may only go up to 1080p — 4K/8K requires the uploader to have rendered it.
HDR formats require the source video to be HDR-encoded.

High-res YouTube streams (1080p+) often have separate video and audio tracks that must be merged by FFmpeg.

Ensure the backend has FFmpeg installed and configured.
Try re-downloading — a merge failure can occasionally produce a video-only file.
For audio only, use the MP3 option instead.

Re-uploading YouTube content to other platforms may be flagged by content ID systems.

Only re-upload content you own or have explicit permission to use.
Check the original video's Creative Commons license.
Lumio is intended for personal offline use only.
